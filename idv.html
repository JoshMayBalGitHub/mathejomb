What are Independent and Dependent Variables?

A Variable is simply a symbol that represents an unknown variable. It is usually represented by letters like x, y, and others. Meanwhile, an Independent Variable is a type of variable that can stand alone and is not dependent on any other variable. It is also referred to as the input of the function. Although, a Dependent Variable is a type of variable that depends on other variables / factors. In other words, it is affected by the changes on the Independent Variable. It is also referred to as the output of the function.

Remember: For example, the variable X is considered as the independent variable because any value could be assigned to it. However, the variable Y is considered the dependent variable because it depends on X's value.

Examples: What are the Independent and Dependent Variables?


1. The teacher needs ink to write on the whiteboard.
The ink of the whiteboard pen
Words / Drawings that can be drawn on the whiteboard

Independent Variable: The ink of the whiteboard pen
Dependent Variable: Words / Drawings that can be drawn on the whiteboard

2. Joshua waters the plant causing it to grow.
Amount of water into the plant
Amount of growth of the plant

Independent Variable: Amount of water into the plant
Dependent Variable: Amount of growth of the plant

3. Angel is considering the amount of words she can type on her computer due to her limited bond paper amount so she can print her essay.
Amount of bond paper
Amount of words typed on the essay

Independent Variable: Amount of water into the plant
Dependent Variable: Amount of growth of the plant

Remember:
We use the example sentence "(Dependent Variable) depends on (Independent Variable)" to determine which of the words / phrases represent. For example: "The amount of video games played depends on the amount of free time the person has".
Independent Variable: Amount of free time
Dependent Variable: Amount of video games played
